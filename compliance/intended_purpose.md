# Intended Purpose — MedScribe AI

## 1. Intended Purpose Statement
MedScribe AI is intended to assist qualified healthcare professionals in creating
clinical documentation by converting spoken consultations and dictation into
structured draft clinical notes through automated transcription and AI-based
language processing.

## 2. Intended Users
- Licensed physicians (fastleger, spesialister)
- Licensed nurses with documentation authority
- Other qualified healthcare professionals authorized to create clinical records
- Healthcare administrative staff under clinical supervision

## 3. Intended Patient Population
- Not applicable — the device does not interact with patients
- The device processes healthcare professional speech about any patient population

## 4. Intended Use Environment
- Primary care clinics (fastlegekontor)
- Hospital departments (sykehusavdelinger)
- Specialist outpatient clinics (poliklinikker)
- Emergency departments (akuttmottak)
- Psychiatric facilities (psykiatriske avdelinger)

## 5. Medical Indication
Clinical documentation workflow support — reducing documentation burden
while maintaining documentation quality and accuracy.

## 6. Contraindications
MedScribe AI must NOT be used:
- As a substitute for clinical judgment, diagnosis, or treatment decisions
- For automated clinical decision-making without human review
- For processing of clinical data without qualified human oversight
- In settings where immediate output is relied upon for emergency treatment decisions

## 7. Essential Performance Requirements
- All outputs must be clearly labeled as "DRAFT" until approved
- Human approval must be required before finalization
- Complete audit trail of all actions must be maintained
- System must fail safely (incomplete output rather than incorrect output)

## 8. Residual Risks (Communicated to User)
- Transcription may contain errors due to audio quality, accents, or background noise
- AI-generated structured notes may contain inaccuracies, omissions, or fabricated information
- The system is not a substitute for professional medical documentation training
- Network or system failures may interrupt documentation workflow
